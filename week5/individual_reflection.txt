Individual reflection

The Expense Tracker project was pretty fun to work on. It was interesting to get into the pandas and matplotlib libraries which I have never worked with before. Reading and writing different types of files, handling errors, analyzing data and generating new and valuable data (both textually and graphyically) as well as writing unit tests was fun. Going from an initially all procedural programming to an OOP style was a little bit of a challenge, because I was not expecting that transition in the middle of the course. Nevertheless, seeing the project grow and writing out more logic made me think once more about the project structure and design. However, I do believe I have made some mistakes in creating my project structure. I have been working alone in this project and I do have a tendency to get tunnel vision and not see other solutions. It would be a very valuable experience and learning moment to see other peoples projects and see what I could have done differently. Thus, I have uploaded my project to my github page (https://github.com/mohafarman/expense_tracker) for others to see and poke around if they wish. Which bring me to my final point, I am skeptical about the outcomes of the project. The expenses are way above the budget targets. As there hasn't been feedback yet on the project assignments, I am not sure whether I have done something wrong.

With that being said. I am now more confident in tackling future projects which require handling large sets of data.
